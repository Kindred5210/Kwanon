# Source Notes

Use these notes as background, not as hard-coded truth. If the user asks for latest figures, browse and verify current sources.

## Public Research Anchors

- OpenAI and University of Pennsylvania's "GPTs are GPTs" work frames LLM impact as task exposure rather than simple job deletion.
- WEF Future of Jobs reporting emphasizes skill disruption and job churn through 2030.
- McKinsey Global Institute estimates generative AI can accelerate automation of work activities and occupational transitions.
- OECD employment analysis stresses uncertainty in net job effects and the importance of task change, skills, and institutions.
- ILO research argues generative AI is more likely to augment many roles before it fully automates entire occupations.
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework is useful for communication principles: transparency, explainability, contestability, and uncertainty.

## Modeling Limits

- There is no reliable dataset that maps one person's biography to an exact probability of being replaced.
- The model should estimate pressure and resilience under scenarios.
- Personal actions can change the curve; the forecast should invite revision every 3-6 months.
- Sensitive attributes should not be treated as destiny or moral value.
